## Boundary divergences (where the same principles land in different clusters)

The disagreements are about where to place the cuts within each region. Specifically, they disagree on five boundary decisions:

1. Whether formal and discursive transparency belong together (G: yes; C: no)
2. Whether epistemic robustness and procedural fairness belong together (G: yes; C: no)
3. Whether systemic coherence is a primitive family or a derived property of robustness and understanding (G: primitive; C: derived)
4. Whether cognitive architecture deserves its own cluster (G: no; C: yes)
5. Whether epistemic conduct is distinct from dialectical norms (G: yes; C: no)

Each disagreement reflects a genuine classificatory choice — whether to lump on the basis of a shared functional question or to split on the basis of distinct disciplinary mechanisms.

Globally, C keeps neighboring zones cleaner, while G regroups cross-cutting items by the work they do in an argument.

### 1. Structural transparency: unified in G, split in C

G merges proof-theoretic structure (canonical form, detour elimination, introduction-rule primacy) with discourse-level transparency (framing, salience hierarchy, procedural reconstructibility) into a single family — **Structural transparency** — on the ground that both concern inspectability.

C separates these into cluster **I** (Formal and Structural Integrity of Inference) and cluster **VII** (Discourse Organization and Transparency). The split reflects a distinction between logical architecture and communicative structure that G deliberately collapses.

### 2. Dialectical content: one family in G, two clusters in C

G's **Dialectical robustness** (family 4) covers both the epistemic side (rebuttal pre-emption, sensitivity to defeaters, strategic completeness) and the procedural-social side (distinction of dialectical roles, resolution criterion, burden-of-proof discipline, fair reconstruction).

C distributes these across cluster **IV** (Robustness and Defeasibility — the epistemic stability of conclusions) and cluster **VIII** (Dialectical and Contextual Norms — the procedural rules of argumentative exchange). The split reflects C's decision to separate _what makes a conclusion resilient_ from _what makes an exchange fair_.

### 3. Systemic coherence: a dedicated family in G, distributed in C

G elevates **Systemic coherence and integration** (family 6) as a separate concern — coherence across claims, local-to-global organization, dependence-structure integration, and organization by deep pattern rather than topic juxtaposition.

C has no dedicated coherence cluster. It places _coherence of the belief system_ in cluster IV (Robustness and Defeasibility) and _coherence integration_ and _grasp of dependence relations_ in cluster VI (Understanding Beyond Knowledge). What G treats as one family, C treats as a by-product of robustness on one hand and understanding on the other.

### 4. Cognitive architecture: a dedicated cluster in C, distributed in G

C elevates **Cognitive Architecture and Processing** (cluster IX) as a distinct concern — mental models, chunking, cognitive economy, causal/structural preference, analogical support, contrastive explanation, retrieval and generativity.

G distributes these: cognitive economy falls under **Inferential relevance and economy** (family 2); chunking appears in both families 2 and 7; analogical support and contrastive explanation fall under **Understanding, intelligibility and transfer** (family 7). G does not recognize a separate cognitive-processing layer.

### 5. Epistemic conduct: a dedicated family in G, absorbed in C

G's **Epistemic conduct** (family 9) isolates the _attitude of the inquirer_ — honesty about uncertainty, fairness to alternatives, discipline against premature closure — as a concern distinct from the inferential architecture of the argument.

C folds these into cluster VIII (Dialectical and Contextual Norms), merging them with procedural norms like resolution criterion and context sensitivity. The dedicated family in G reflects a judgment that epistemic virtue is not reducible to procedural compliance; C treats it as part of the same dialectical package.

### 6. Pedagogy and knowledge construction: one family in G, two clusters in C

G's **Pedagogical exposition** (family 8) covers audience adaptation, sequencing, scaffolding, worked examples, misconception management, and salience hierarchy in a single family.

C splits this into cluster **VII** (which absorbs progressive disclosure alongside discourse organization) and cluster **X** (Knowledge Construction and Transfer — scaffolding, worked examples, misconception management, feedback, transfer orientation). The split separates _how discourse is organized for readability_ from _how knowledge is built and transferred across contexts_.
